Practice Torque Problems

Problem 1: In the testing of the brakes on a bicycle wheel, the wheel is mounted to a fixed axle with negligible friction. The wheel has a rotational inertia of 0.12 kg · m2, and the coefficient of friction between the rubber brake pads and the metal rim is 0.41. The wheel is initially spinning with an angular velocity of 27 rad/s. If the two brake pads each exert a force of 2.0 N on the rim at a distance of 34 cm from the center of rotation, determine the time it takes the wheel to stop.

  • a. 4.6 s
  • b. 5.8 s
  • c. 12 s
  • d. 19 s

Problem 2: A wrench 0.25 m long is used to apply a force of 50 N at its end. What is the torque applied to the bolt?

  • a. 10 N·m
  • b. 12.5 N·m
  • c. 15 N·m
  • d. 20 N·m

Problem 3: A uniform rod of length 2 m and weight 30 N is pivoted at one end. A 20 N weight is hung at the other end. What is the net torque about the pivot?

  • a. 10 N·m
  • b. 40 N·m
  • c. 50 N·m
  • d. 60 N·m

Problem 4: A horizontal force of 100 N is applied to a door 0.9 m wide. If the force is applied perpendicular to the door, what is the torque about the hinge?

  • a. 80 N·m
  • b. 85 N·m
  • c. 90 N·m
  • d. 95 N·m

Problem 5: A force of 25 N is applied at an angle of 30° to a lever arm 0.5 m long. What is the torque generated?

  • a. 6.25 N·m
  • b. 7.5 N·m
  • c. 10.8 N·m
  • d. 12.5 N·m
